About This exhibit

— Theme —

The San Diego & Arizona Eastern RR is a realistic HO scale layout modeled on southeast California in the 1950s and heads into Mexico. The layout has 15 scale miles of mainline and follows the California-Mexico border before it crosses over into Mexico at Tecate and back out in southern San Diego.

— Train Control —

The layout is controlled by DC and DCC systems that change each day. The DCC system uses NCE controllers to control the speed, sound, and lights in a train from either an app on your phone or a controller that you can plug in at many points around the layout using an AC current. In DC mode, engineers can control their trains using one of four controllers that use a DC current (hence the name DC mode) to move the trains back and forth. In DC mode, you aren't able to control the sounds of trains or their lights. There are also switchboards mounted under the layout to change the course of the trains using the above-ground switches.
